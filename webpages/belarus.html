Discover Belarus

About Belarus

Belarus, located in Eastern Europe, is a land of vast forests, scenic lakes, and deep cultural roots. Known for its unspoiled nature and Soviet-era architecture, Belarus offers a unique glimpse into both past and present Europe. The capital, Minsk, is a modern city with grand boulevards and vibrant cultural life.

Did you know? About 40% of Belarus is covered by forests, and it is home to Europe's largest remaining primeval forest — the Białowieża Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage Site!

Geography

Belarus features serene natural landscapes:

  • The Białowieża Forest, home to the rare European bison
  • Numerous lakes and rivers, including Lake Narach, the largest in the country
  • Rolling hills and fertile plains perfect for agriculture
  • National parks like Pripyatsky and Braslav Lakes

Culture

Belarusian culture is a blend of tradition, resilience, and creativity:

  • Rich folklore with traditional music, dance, and embroidery
  • Historic castles such as Mir Castle and Nesvizh Palace
  • Traditional dishes like draniki (potato pancakes) and machanka (pork stew)
  • Major festivals like Kupalle (summer solstice celebration)

Tourism Highlights

Top attractions in Belarus include:

  • Minsk’s Independence Avenue and Victory Square
  • The medieval town of Polotsk, one of the oldest cities in Belarus
  • The Brest Hero-Fortress, commemorating WWII history
  • The open-air Museum of Folk Architecture and Rural Lifestyle near Minsk
  • Exploring the Braslav Lakes region for boating and nature watching
