Discover Belarus

A Land of Forests and Fortresses

About Belarus

Belarus is an Eastern European country known for its lush forests, grand castles, and Soviet-era architecture. It shares borders with Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Lithuania, and Latvia. The capital city, Minsk, is a modern hub with historical and cultural depth.

Belarus is often described as a hidden gem, with peaceful countryside, historic towns, and a rich folk culture that has been preserved over centuries.

Did you know? Around 40% of Belarus is covered by forests, making it one of Europe’s greenest countries!

Geography

Belarus features expansive lowlands, marshes, and rivers:

  • Vast woodlands like the Białowieża Forest, home to the European bison
  • Rivers such as the Dnieper and Neman flow through the country
  • Fertile soil and green fields dominate the rural areas
  • Cold winters and warm summers create diverse natural beauty

Culture

Belarusian culture is rooted in Slavic traditions with a strong sense of national pride:

  • Belarusian and Russian are both official languages
  • Traditional folk dances, embroidery, and music remain vibrant
  • Local cuisine includes dishes like draniki (potato pancakes) and machanka (meat stew)
  • Soviet monuments and Orthodox churches highlight the nation's mixed heritage

Tourism Highlights

Discover Belarus through its iconic sites:

Mir Castle

A UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles.

Białowieża Forest

One of Europe’s last primeval forests and a haven for wildlife enthusiasts.

Minsk

The capital blends Soviet architecture with green spaces, museums, and nightlife.
