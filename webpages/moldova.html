Discover Moldova

About Moldova

Moldova, a landlocked country in Eastern Europe, is known for its rich cultural heritage, lush landscapes, and friendly people. Despite being one of the least known countries in Europe, Moldova is home to beautiful vineyards, ancient monasteries, and a fascinating history. The capital, Chișinău, is an energetic city with a mix of Soviet-era architecture and modern influences.

Did you know? Moldova is one of the world's largest wine producers, with an underground wine city in Cricova, where wine cellars stretch over 120 kilometers!

Geography

Moldova features varied landscapes, from rolling hills to beautiful vineyards:

  • The rolling hills of the Codru region, which is home to the country’s most famous vineyards
  • The Dniester River, which forms the eastern border of the country
  • The Orheiul Vechi, an ancient complex of monasteries and caves carved into the rock
  • The southern part of the country, known for its fertile plains and agricultural richness

Culture

Moldovan culture is deeply influenced by its historical ties to Romania and Russia, with a blend of traditional customs and modern-day influences:

  • Rich folk traditions, including dance, music, and intricate folk costumes
  • The famous Moldovan wine-making tradition, dating back to the ancient Dacian times
  • Traditional cuisine, including dishes like mămăligă (cornmeal porridge), plăcinte (pastries), and sarmale (stuffed cabbage rolls)
  • The celebration of the holiday of "Mărțișor", welcoming the arrival of spring with colorful amulets

Tourism Highlights

Some of Moldova's must-see destinations include:

  • The Cricova Wine Cellars, one of the largest wine complexes in Europe
  • Orheiul Vechi, an archaeological and historical complex offering stunning views
  • The Stefan Cel Mare Park and Memorial Complex in Chișinău
  • The Capriana Monastery, one of the oldest monasteries in Moldova
  • The village of Butuceni, known for its traditional stone houses and guesthouses
