About North Macedonia

North Macedonia, a hidden gem in the heart of the Balkans, is a country of ancient history, breathtaking landscapes, and a vibrant cultural mix. Its capital, Skopje, blends Ottoman heritage with modern monuments, while Lake Ohrid—one of Europe's oldest lakes—offers stunning natural beauty.

Did you know? North Macedonia was part of the ancient kingdom of Macedon and is home to numerous archaeological sites, including the birthplace of Alexander the Great.

Geography

North Macedonia boasts diverse geography, featuring:

  • Lake Ohrid – A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its crystal-clear waters and medieval churches
  • Shar Mountains – Perfect for hiking and winter sports
  • Vardar River Valley – The country's agricultural heartland
  • Matka Canyon – A stunning gorge with caves and monasteries near Skopje

Culture

North Macedonian culture is a rich blend of influences:

  • Famous for its traditional folk music and dances, such as the oro (circle dance)
  • Renowned for its Orthodox Christian monasteries with exquisite frescoes
  • Delicious cuisine featuring tavče gravče (baked beans), ajvar (pepper relish), and pastrmajlija (meat pie)
  • Celebrations like Ilinden (a national holiday) and Vevčani Carnival (a quirky folk festival)

Tourism Highlights

Must-see attractions in North Macedonia include:

  • Ohrid – A UNESCO-listed town with ancient churches and lakeside charm
  • Skopje – The capital, known for its Stone Bridge, Old Bazaar, and grand statues
  • Mavrovo National Park – Ideal for nature lovers and skiing
  • Heraclea Lyncestis – Roman ruins near Bitola
  • Stobi – An impressive archaeological site from antiquity
