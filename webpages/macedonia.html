Discover North Macedonia

About North Macedonia

North Macedonia, a landlocked country in the heart of the Balkans, is known for its picturesque landscapes, ancient ruins, and rich cultural heritage. The capital, Skopje, is a blend of modernity and history, with its Ottoman-era bazaars and newly constructed landmarks. The country's ancient history, dating back to the time of Alexander the Great, is evident throughout its monuments, fortresses, and UNESCO World Heritage sites.

Did you know? North Macedonia is home to Lake Ohrid, one of the oldest and deepest lakes in Europe, shared with Albania, and is a UNESCO World Heritage site!

Geography

North Macedonia offers a diverse range of natural landscapes, from its towering mountains to tranquil lakes:

  • The stunning Lake Ohrid, famous for its crystal-clear waters and historic monasteries
  • The UNESCO-protected Pelister National Park, known for its beautiful landscapes and wildlife
  • The Vardar River, which runs through the country, providing fertile plains and picturesque views
  • The Shar Mountains, offering hiking opportunities and spectacular panoramas

Culture

The culture of North Macedonia is shaped by its history and diverse population, with influences from Greek, Ottoman, and Slavic traditions:

  • The traditional Macedonian music and dance, including the famous "oro" circle dance
  • Delicious Macedonian cuisine, with specialties like tavče gravče (baked beans) and ajvar (pepper spread)
  • The Orthodox Christian tradition, especially evident in the beautiful churches and monasteries, like the Church of St. Panteleimon in Ohrid
  • The celebration of the Slava, a family tradition unique to the Orthodox Christian Slavs of the region

Tourism Highlights

Some of the best tourist attractions in North Macedonia include:

  • Lake Ohrid and its UNESCO-listed Old Town with ancient churches and monasteries
  • The ancient city of Stobi, with Roman ruins and well-preserved mosaics
  • The town of Bitola, with its Ottoman architecture and lively cafes
  • The Skopje Fortress (Kale) offering stunning views over the capital
  • The beautiful Matka Canyon, known for its caves, monasteries, and stunning scenery
