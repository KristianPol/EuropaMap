Welcome to North Macedonia

About North Macedonia

North Macedonia, officially known as the Republic of North Macedonia, is a landlocked country in Southeast Europe, known for its rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural landscapes. The capital city, Skopje, is a vibrant hub of culture and history, while the country is also famous for its ancient ruins, picturesque lakes, and traditional festivals.

Geography

North Macedonia is bordered by Kosovo to the northwest, Serbia to the north, Bulgaria to the east, Greece to the south, and Albania to the west. The country is characterized by its mountainous terrain, with the Šar Mountains and the Osogovo-Belasica mountain range dominating the landscape. North Macedonia is also home to Lake Ohrid, one of the oldest and deepest lakes in Europe, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Culture

North Macedonia has a rich cultural heritage influenced by its history as part of the Ottoman Empire and its Slavic roots. The country is known for its traditional music, dance, and festivals, as well as its Orthodox Christian monasteries and churches. Macedonian cuisine is a blend of Mediterranean and Balkan flavors, with dishes like Tavče Gravče (bean stew) and Ajvar (pepper relish) being popular staples.

Tourism

North Macedonia is a growing tourist destination, offering a mix of natural beauty, historic landmarks, and cultural experiences. Popular attractions include Lake Ohrid, the ancient city of Heraclea Lyncestis, the Matka Canyon, and the vibrant capital city of Skopje. Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, exploring ancient ruins, and experiencing traditional Macedonian hospitality.
