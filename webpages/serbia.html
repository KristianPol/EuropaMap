About Serbia

Serbia, located at the crossroads of Central and Southeast Europe, is a country with a rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. Its capital Belgrade, one of Europe's oldest cities, sits at the confluence of the Danube and Sava rivers, offering a unique mix of historical heritage and modern energy.

Did you know? Serbia is home to the world's largest Orthodox church, the Church of Saint Sava in Belgrade, and the ancient Roman city of Felix Romuliana, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Geography

Serbia features diverse landscapes including:

  • The Đavolja Varoš (Devil's Town) - a peculiar rock formation
  • The Iron Gates - the longest gorge in Europe on the Danube River
  • Fertile plains of Vojvodina in the north
  • Mountain ranges including Kopaonik and Zlatibor

Culture

Serbian culture is rich in traditions:

  • Famous for its hospitality and traditional family gatherings
  • Unique folk music with ancient gusle instrument
  • Traditional cuisine featuring ćevapi, sarma, and ajvar
  • Celebration of Slava - a family saint's day unique to Serbian Orthodox tradition

Tourism Highlights

Must-see attractions in Serbia include:

  • Belgrade Fortress and Kalemegdan Park
  • Studenica Monastery (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • Novi Sad and the Petrovaradin Fortress
  • Šargan Eight heritage railway
  • Zlatibor and Kopaonik mountain resorts
