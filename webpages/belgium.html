Discover Belgium

A Cultural Crossroads in the Heart of Europe

About Belgium

Belgium is a Western European country known for its medieval towns, Renaissance architecture, and role as headquarters of the European Union and NATO. The capital, Brussels, is home to beautiful squares, chocolate shops, and iconic buildings like the Atomium.

Belgium is a trilingual country with Dutch, French, and German as official languages, reflecting its cultural and regional diversity.

Did you know? Belgium is the birthplace of the saxophone and famous for its more than 1,000 varieties of beer!

Geography

Belgium features a mix of coastal plains, rolling hills, and dense forests:

  • Northern Flanders is flat with urban centers and canals
  • Southern Wallonia includes the Ardennes Forest and scenic rivers
  • The Meuse and Scheldt rivers flow through Belgium
  • Rolling hills and valleys are perfect for hiking and cycling

Culture

Belgian culture is a vibrant blend of languages, art, and gastronomy:

  • Multilingual society with Dutch, French, and German speakers
  • Home of famous artists like Magritte and Bruegel
  • Known for waffles, chocolate, beer, and fries
  • Rich history seen in castles, cathedrals, and battlefields

Tourism Highlights

Explore Belgium through these top destinations:

Bruges

A fairy-tale medieval town with canals, cobbled streets, and Gothic architecture.

Brussels

The capital, known for the Grand Place, Atomium, and world-class museums.

Ghent

A lively university city with a blend of modern life and historic charm.
