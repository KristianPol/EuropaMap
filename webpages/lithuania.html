Discover Lithuania

The Baltic Gem of Europe

About Lithuania

Lithuania, the southernmost Baltic state, is a country of rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. With its capital Vilnius boasting one of Europe's largest medieval old towns, Lithuania offers a perfect blend of historical charm and modern vitality.

Did you know? Lithuania was the last pagan country in Europe, converting to Christianity only in 1387. The country still maintains many of its ancient traditions and folklore.

Geography

Lithuania features diverse landscapes including:

  • The Curonian Spit - a UNESCO World Heritage site with unique sand dunes
  • Over 3,000 lakes, perfect for summer recreation
  • Dense forests covering about 33% of the country
  • The Nemunas River - Lithuania's largest river

Culture

Lithuanian culture is rich in traditions:

  • Famous for its basketball passion (considered the second religion)
  • Unique folk music with ancient polyphonic sutartinės songs
  • Traditional cuisine featuring cepelinai (potato dumplings) and šaltibarščiai (cold beet soup)
  • Celebration of Joninės (Midsummer) with special rituals

Tourism Highlights

Must-see attractions in Lithuania include:

  • Vilnius Old Town (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • Trakai Island Castle
  • Hill of Crosses near Šiauliai
  • Curonian Spit National Park
  • Palanga Amber Museum
