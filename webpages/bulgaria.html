About Bulgaria

Bulgaria, located in the southeastern part of Europe, is a country steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. From its ancient Thracian heritage to its iconic Black Sea coastline, Bulgaria is a diverse country with rich traditions. Its capital, Sofia, is one of Europe’s oldest cities, blending Roman ruins, Orthodox churches, and a modern cosmopolitan atmosphere.

Did you know? Bulgaria is the birthplace of the Cyrillic alphabet, and it has over 3,000 years of history, including the famous Thracian tombs, which are UNESCO World Heritage sites!

Geography

Bulgaria's geography is incredibly diverse, ranging from mountains to plains and coastlines:

  • The Balkan Mountains, which divide the country into northern and southern regions
  • The Black Sea coast, offering beautiful beaches and resorts like Varna and Burgas
  • The Rila and Pirin mountains, home to breathtaking landscapes and skiing resorts
  • The Danube River, which forms part of Bulgaria’s northern border

Culture

Bulgaria has a deep cultural heritage influenced by ancient traditions and modern European life:

  • The famous Rose Festival in Kazanlak, celebrating the country’s centuries-old tradition of rose oil production
  • The traditional Bulgarian folk music and dances, such as the "horo" dance
  • Delicious Bulgarian cuisine, with dishes like banitsa (pastry), shopska salad, and moussaka
  • Fascinating religious traditions, especially the celebrations surrounding Easter and Christmas

Tourism Highlights

Bulgaria offers a variety of attractions for travelers to explore:

  • The ancient city of Plovdiv, with its Roman theatre and cobblestone streets
  • The Rila Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage site and an important cultural landmark
  • Varna and the Black Sea coast, with its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife
  • The Thracian Tomb of Kazanlak, famous for its frescoes and history
  • The Seven Rila Lakes, a stunning natural wonder in the Rila Mountains
