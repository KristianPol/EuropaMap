Discover Hungary

A Land of Thermal Spas, History, and the Danube

About Hungary

Hungary, located in Central Europe, is a country rich in culture, history, and architectural beauty. It’s known for its vibrant capital Budapest, traditional thermal baths, and unique language and cuisine.

Budapest, split by the Danube River, offers a striking contrast of historic Buda and modern Pest.

Did you know? Hungary is home to one of the oldest metro lines in the world—Line 1 of Budapest's subway system opened in 1896!

Geography

Hungary features a mix of flat plains, rolling hills, and rivers:

  • The Danube and Tisza rivers flow through the country
  • Home to Lake Balaton, Central Europe’s largest lake
  • Abundant thermal springs and bathhouses
  • National parks and protected areas like Hortobágy

Culture

Hungarian culture is deeply influenced by its history, music, and cuisine:

  • Folk music and classical composers like Liszt and Bartók
  • Rich cuisine including goulash, paprika dishes, and chimney cake
  • Stunning architecture from Gothic to Art Nouveau
  • A unique language and a deep literary tradition

Tourism Highlights

Top places to visit in Hungary include:

Buda Castle

Historic palace complex with panoramic views over Budapest.

Széchenyi Thermal Bath

One of Europe's largest and most famous thermal bath complexes.

Lake Balaton

A popular summer destination with beaches, vineyards, and sailing.
