Discover Luxembourg

About Luxembourg

Luxembourg is a small, landlocked country located in Western Europe. Known for its historical landmarks, luxurious castles, and as a hub of European finance, Luxembourg blends ancient tradition with modern influence. Its capital, Luxembourg City, offers an exceptional view into Europe’s political and cultural heart.

Did you know? Luxembourg has the highest GDP per capita in the world!

Geography

Luxembourg is characterized by lush forests, rolling hills, and charming villages:

  • The Ardennes Forest, with its stunning natural beauty and wildlife
  • The picturesque Moselle Valley, famous for vineyards and scenic rivers
  • Luxembourg City, built on a dramatic cliff with beautiful fortifications
  • The Mullerthal region, known as “Little Switzerland” for its unique rock formations

Culture

Luxembourg has a rich culture influenced by its European neighbors, offering a blend of French, German, and Luxembourgish traditions:

  • Luxembourgish is the official language, although French and German are also widely spoken
  • Luxembourg is famous for its festivals, including the vibrant Schueberfouer
  • The Grand Ducal Palace, home to the Grand Duke, is a symbol of Luxembourg’s history
  • Traditional cuisine such as Judd mat Gaardebounen (smoked pork with beans) and Gromperekichelcher (fried potato cakes)

Tourism Highlights

Some of the must-see places in Luxembourg include:

  • The historical Luxembourg City, with its UNESCO-listed old town
  • Vianden Castle, a fairy-tale fortress nestled in the Ardennes
  • The American Cementery Memorial, dedicated to U.S. soldiers from World War II
  • Mullerthal Trail, a hiker’s paradise in the “Little Switzerland” region
  • Clervaux Castle and its impressive photographic exhibitions
