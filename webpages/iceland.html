Discover Iceland

About Iceland

Iceland, known as the Land of Fire and Ice, is a Nordic island nation characterized by its dramatic landscapes of volcanoes, geysers, hot springs, and lava fields. Its capital, Reykjavik, runs on geothermal power and is home to a vibrant cultural scene and stunning architecture.

Did you know? Iceland is one of the most sparsely populated countries in Europe, and it's home to the largest glacier in Europe — Vatnajökull — as well as some of the world's most active volcanoes.

Geography

Iceland’s natural wonders include:

  • The Blue Lagoon - a famous geothermal spa
  • The Golden Circle - including Þingvellir National Park, Geysir, and Gullfoss waterfall
  • Vatnajökull Glacier and its stunning ice caves
  • Volcanic landscapes such as Eyjafjallajökull

Culture

Icelandic culture is deeply rooted in its history and natural environment:

  • Strong traditions in storytelling and sagas
  • Vibrant music scene featuring artists like Björk and Sigur Rós
  • Unique cuisine including skyr, lamb, and seafood dishes
  • Celebrations like Þorrablót, a midwinter feast

Tourism Highlights

Top attractions in Iceland include:

  • Reykjavik’s Hallgrímskirkja Church
  • The Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis)
  • Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon
  • Snæfellsnes Peninsula – “Iceland in Miniature”
  • Whale watching tours from Húsavík
