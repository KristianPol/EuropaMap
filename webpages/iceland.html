Discover Iceland

Land of Fire, Ice, and Natural Wonders

About Iceland

Iceland, a Nordic island nation, is renowned for its dramatic landscapes of volcanoes, geysers, glaciers, and hot springs. It has a small population but a big reputation for natural beauty and sustainable living.

Its capital, Reykjavík, is the northernmost capital in the world and is famous for its modern culture and proximity to nature.

Did you know? Iceland runs almost entirely on renewable energy—geothermal and hydroelectric power!

Geography

Iceland’s geography is shaped by volcanic and glacial activity:

  • Over 30 active volcanoes
  • Home to Vatnajökull, Europe’s largest glacier
  • Hot springs and geysers scattered across the country
  • Surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean with stunning fjords

Culture

Icelandic culture is rooted in Norse traditions and modern innovation:

  • Rich in sagas and medieval literature
  • Unique folklore filled with elves and trolls
  • A thriving music scene with artists like Björk and Sigur Rós
  • Traditional foods include skyr, lamb, and seafood

Tourism Highlights

Top places to visit in Iceland include:

Blue Lagoon

A world-famous geothermal spa set in a lava field.

Golden Circle

A popular tourist route featuring Thingvellir, Geysir, and Gullfoss waterfall.

Northern Lights

A magical natural phenomenon best viewed in winter.
