Discover Bosnia and Herzegovina

About Bosnia and Herzegovina

Bosnia and Herzegovina is a country located in the western Balkans, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, medieval towns, and rich cultural history. The capital, Sarajevo, is a city with a vibrant blend of Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, and Yugoslav influences, making it a unique place to visit in Europe.

Did you know? Bosnia and Herzegovina is home to the famous Old Bridge of Mostar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and is also known for its incredible landscapes, such as the Sutjeska National Park, home to the last remaining primeval forest in Europe.

Geography

Bosnia and Herzegovina has diverse landscapes and natural beauty:

  • The stunning Neretva River and its famous Old Bridge in Mostar
  • The rugged Dinaric Alps, perfect for hiking and mountaineering
  • The beautiful lakes and waterfalls in Una National Park
  • The tranquil and historic city of Višegrad, home to the UNESCO-listed Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge

Culture

Bosnian culture is an incredible blend of East and West, influenced by Ottoman, Slavic, and European traditions:

  • The famous Bosnian coffee, a social ritual with a deep cultural significance
  • Traditional cuisine, including ćevapi, burek, and the famous pita
  • Rich folk music traditions, including the sevdalinka genre, which is a soulful, slow form of music
  • The Sarajevo Film Festival, one of the most prestigious film festivals in the Balkans

Tourism Highlights

Top attractions in Bosnia and Herzegovina include:

  • The Old Bridge in Mostar (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • Sarajevo’s Baščaršija, an Ottoman-era bazaar
  • The stunning waterfalls of Kravica
  • Jajce, a medieval town known for its picturesque waterfall and historic fortresses
  • The Sutjeska National Park, home to the primeval forest of Perućica
