Discover Bosnia and Herzegovina

A Crossroads of Cultures and Landscapes

About Bosnia and Herzegovina

Bosnia and Herzegovina is located in Southeast Europe on the Balkan Peninsula. It's known for its rich history, multicultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty. Sarajevo, the capital, is famous for its diverse religious sites and its role in 20th-century history.

The country has a unique mix of Eastern and Western influences, evident in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions.

Did you know? The iconic Stari Most (Old Bridge) in Mostar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of reconciliation.

Geography

The landscape of Bosnia and Herzegovina is mountainous, green, and full of rivers:

  • The Dinaric Alps dominate the terrain, ideal for hiking and skiing
  • Rivers like the Neretva and Drina shape valleys and canyons
  • Dense forests and nature parks support rich biodiversity
  • The country has a small coastline on the Adriatic Sea in Neum
