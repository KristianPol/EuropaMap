Discover Andorra

About Andorra

Andorra, a small principality nestled in the Pyrenees mountains between France and Spain, is famous for its stunning alpine scenery, charming medieval towns, and tax-free shopping. Despite its small size, Andorra is a vibrant country with a rich history and a unique blend of Catalan and French influences. Its capital, Andorra la Vella, is the highest capital city in Europe.

Did you know? Andorra is known for having one of the lowest unemployment rates in Europe, and it’s a top destination for ski enthusiasts in the winter months!

Geography

Andorra’s geography is characterized by steep mountains, valleys, and scenic vistas:

  • The Pyrenees Mountains offer fantastic skiing opportunities in the winter and hiking trails in the summer
  • The Madriu-Perafita-Claror Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a protected natural area with scenic landscapes
  • The Vallnord and Grandvalira ski resorts, known for their world-class slopes and views
  • The Ordino Arcalís Valley, a popular area for outdoor sports throughout the year

Culture

Andorran culture is a mix of Catalan, Spanish, and French influences, reflected in its language, food, and traditions:

  • The official language is Catalan, although Spanish, French, and Portuguese are widely spoken
  • Famous for its music festivals, such as the Andorra la Vella International Music Festival
  • Andorran cuisine includes traditional Catalan dishes like trinxat (potato and cabbage dish) and escudella (meat and vegetable stew)
  • The country celebrates unique cultural events, like the traditional Andorran folk dances during festivals

Tourism Highlights

Andorra offers a wide range of activities and attractions:

  • The ski resorts of Grandvalira and Vallnord, perfect for skiing and snowboarding
  • The Romanesque churches, including Sant Joan de Caselles and Santa Coloma, showcasing the country’s medieval heritage
  • The Caldea Spa, a large spa complex offering thermal baths and wellness treatments
  • The charming village of Ordino, with its picturesque streets and traditional alpine architecture
  • Shopping in Andorra la Vella, known for its duty-free stores
