Discover Albania

About Albania

Albania, a beautiful country located in Southeast Europe on the Adriatic and Ionian Seas, is known for its stunning beaches, mountains, and ancient history. The capital city, Tirana, is a dynamic blend of vibrant culture, modern art, and traditional Albanian heritage. Albania has a rich history dating back to ancient Illyrian, Greek, and Roman civilizations, which can be explored through its archaeological sites and ancient monuments.

Did you know? Albania is home to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Gjirokastër, an ancient city known for its well-preserved Ottoman architecture!

Geography

Albania offers a diverse landscape of pristine beaches, rugged mountains, and fertile plains:

  • The Albanian Riviera, famous for its crystal-clear waters and beautiful sandy beaches
  • The Accursed Mountains (Prokletije) offer some of the most scenic views and trekking routes in the Balkans
  • The Shkodra Lake, the largest lake in the Balkans, shared with Montenegro
  • The Ionian and Adriatic Seas, with beautiful beaches like Dhërmi, Jale, and Ksamil

Culture

Albania’s culture is a unique blend of ancient traditions, folklore, and Mediterranean influences:

  • The Albanian language, one of the oldest languages in Europe, spoken by the majority of the population
  • Traditional music and dance, including the famous "iso-polyphony" singing style, recognized by UNESCO
  • Delicious Albanian cuisine, such as byrek (savory pastry), tave kosi (lamb with yogurt), and raki (fruit brandy)
  • Rich religious diversity, with both Muslim and Christian communities coexisting peacefully for centuries

Tourism Highlights

Some of the top attractions in Albania include:

  • The ancient city of Butrint, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its Greek, Roman, and Byzantine ruins
  • The historic town of Gjirokastër, with its well-preserved Ottoman-era houses and castle
  • The Llogara Pass, offering breathtaking views of the Ionian Sea and surrounding mountains
  • The beaches of Ksamil, known for their turquoise waters and sandy shores
  • The city of Shkodra, with its impressive Rozafa Castle and rich history
