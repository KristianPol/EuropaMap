About Romania

Romania, located in the heart of Eastern Europe, is a country with a rich history, diverse landscapes, and a vibrant culture. Known for its picturesque castles, including the famous Bran Castle (often associated with the Dracula legend), Romania is a destination that offers much more, from bustling cities to tranquil rural villages and breathtaking natural beauty.

Did you know? Romania is home to the Transfagarasan Highway, one of the most beautiful and scenic roads in the world, as well as the ancient Dacian Fortresses, a UNESCO World Heritage site!

Geography

Romania boasts a variety of landscapes:

  • The Carpathian Mountains, ideal for hiking, skiing, and nature lovers
  • The Danube River, which forms part of the country's southern border
  • The stunning Black Sea coast, with resorts like Mamaia and Constanța
  • The Danube Delta, one of Europe's largest and most biodiverse wetlands

Culture

Romanian culture is a blend of Latin, Slavic, Ottoman, and Hungarian influences, offering a rich tapestry of traditions and customs:

  • The famous Romanian hospitality and the tradition of the "Mărțișor" festival, marking the arrival of spring
  • Folk music, with instruments like the pan flute and the violin, and lively dances such as the hora
  • Romanian cuisine, featuring dishes like sarmale (stuffed cabbage rolls), mici (grilled sausages), and a variety of cheeses
  • The vibrant traditions of Easter and Christmas, including intricate painted eggs and caroling

Tourism Highlights

Must-visit attractions in Romania include:

  • Bran Castle, famously associated with Dracula
  • The medieval town of Sibiu, with its stunning old town and cultural heritage
  • Peleș Castle in Sinaia, a neo-Renaissance gem
  • The Transfagarasan Highway, one of the world’s most scenic drives
  • The beautiful seaside city of Constanța and the Black Sea resorts
