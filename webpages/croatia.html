About Croatia

Croatia, located along the Adriatic Sea in Southeast Europe, is a country known for its stunning coastline, medieval cities, and rich cultural heritage. The capital city, Zagreb, is a vibrant urban center, while the coastline is dotted with beautiful islands and ancient towns like Dubrovnik, often referred to as the "Pearl of the Adriatic."

Did you know? Croatia is home to eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the historic town of Dubrovnik and the stunning Plitvice Lakes National Park!

Geography

Croatia offers diverse and scenic landscapes:

  • The breathtaking Dalmatian coast, with its crystal-clear waters and beautiful islands
  • The UNESCO-listed Plitvice Lakes National Park, known for its stunning waterfalls and turquoise lakes
  • The Istrian Peninsula, famous for its ancient Roman ruins and charming hilltop towns
  • The picturesque city of Split, with its historic Diocletian’s Palace

Culture

Croatian culture is steeped in traditions and history:

  • Rich folk music, including the traditional klapa singing style from Dalmatia
  • Croatian cuisine, including dishes like peka, ćevapi, and seafood from the Adriatic coast
  • The famous Dubrovnik Summer Festival, celebrating arts, music, and theater
  • Historical landmarks, such as the Roman amphitheater in Pula and the Diocletian’s Palace in Split

Tourism Highlights

Top destinations in Croatia include:

  • Dubrovnik’s Old Town and City Walls (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • The stunning Plitvice Lakes National Park
  • The ancient town of Split and Diocletian’s Palace
  • The beautiful island of Hvar, known for its beaches and nightlife
  • The historic city of Pula, home to a Roman amphitheater
